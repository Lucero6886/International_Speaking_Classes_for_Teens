📱 Tech & Social Media

Let's talk about apps, screen time, and our digital lives!

👥 Teens (13-17) 📊 Mixed A2-B1 ⏱️ 60 Minutes 💻 Zoom Class

🎯 Today's Goals (By the end of this lesson, you can...):

  1. Name and describe common social media habits.
  2. Express opinions about the pros and cons of screen time.
  3. Compare different apps and give reasons for your preferences.

⏱️ Teaching Flow:

  • 1. Warm-up (5 min)
  • 2. Vocabulary (8 min)
  • 3. Guided Practice (8 min)
  • 4. Main Speaking (15 min)
  • 5. Breakout Task (12 min)
  • 6. Quick Quiz (5 min)
  • 7. Reflection (5 min)
  • 8. Wrap-up (2 min)

🔥 1. Warm-up: Digital Check-In

📹 ZOOM TIP Have students type their app usage (e.g., "3 hours") in the chat, then call on 2-3 volunteers to expand.

1. How much time do you spend on your phone every day? ⏱️
A2 Core I use my phone for about 3 hours a day.
B1 Extended I probably spend around 4 hours daily, mostly watching videos or chatting with friends.
2. What is your favorite app right now? 📱
A2 Core My favorite app is TikTok because it's funny.
B1 Extended Currently, my favorite app is Instagram because I like to see what my friends are doing.
3. Do you prefer texting or calling your friends? 💬📞
A2 Core I prefer texting. It is fast and easy.
B1 Extended I definitely prefer texting because I can reply when I have free time. Calling makes me a bit nervous.
4. Do you ever feel stressed by notifications? 🔔
A2 Core Sometimes, yes. I get too many messages.
B1 Extended Yes, especially when I'm studying. That's why I often put my phone on "Do Not Disturb" mode.
5. Could you live without your smartphone for a week? 🏝️
A2 Core No, I can't. I need it for school and games.
B1 Extended It would be very hard. I use it for everything: alarms, music, maps, and talking to my parents.

📚 2. Tech Vocabulary

🎤 ZOOM TIP Do a choral repetition drill. Have everyone unmute at once to say the words out loud together.

Scroll 👆
(SKROHL) - Verb

Meaning: To move text or pictures up or down on a screen.

A2
"I usually scroll through Instagram before I go to sleep."
Notification 🔔
(no-ti-fi-KAY-shun) - Noun

Meaning: A message or sound telling you something new happened on your app.

A2
"My phone vibrates every time I get a new notification."
Screen time ⏱️
(SKREEN TIME) - Noun

Meaning: The amount of time you spend looking at a phone, tablet, or TV.

A2
"I try to limit my screen time to two hours a day."
Addicted to 😵‍💫
(uh-DIK-ted to) - Adjective

Meaning: Unable to stop doing or using something.

B1
"Many teenagers are addicted to playing online games."
Go viral 🚀
(go VY-rul) - Verb Phrase

Meaning: When a video or post becomes very popular very quickly.

B1
"Her dance video went viral and got a million views."
Distracting 👀
(dis-TRAK-ting) - Adjective

Meaning: Taking your attention away from what you should be doing.

B1
"Listening to music while studying can be very distracting."
Stay connected 🤝
(stay kuh-NEK-ted) - Verb Phrase

Meaning: To keep in touch with friends or family.

A2
"Social media helps me stay connected with my cousins in another city."
Unplug 🔌
(un-PLUG) - Verb

Meaning: To stop using technology and electronics for a while to relax.

B1
"On Sundays, I like to unplug and read a real book."

🧩 3. Sentence Builders

Practice giving reasons using these frames.

Level 1: Basic App Usage (A2)

"I usually use [App] to [Action]."

Example: "I usually use Spotify to listen to music while I study."

Your turn! Use these ideas:
share photos watch funny videos chat with friends learn new things

Level 2: Weighing Pros & Cons (B1)

"The best thing about [App] is that it's [Positive Point], but it can be [Negative Adjective]."

Example: "The best thing about TikTok is that it's entertaining, but it can be very distracting."

Your turn! Use these ideas:
Positive: fun / useful / creative Negative: fake / distracting / addictive

🎤 4. Let's Discuss!

💬 ZOOM TIP Give students 60 seconds of silent thinking time before calling on names.

1. Which apps do you use the most every day, and why? 📱
Easy stay connected entertaining
A2: I use YouTube the most. I use it to watch gaming videos because it is fun.
B1: The app I use the most is Instagram. I like it because it helps me stay connected with my friends, and I enjoy looking at photos.
2. Do you think teenagers spend too much time on their phones? Why? ⏳
Medium addicted to screen time forget real life
A2: Yes, I think so. Many teens play games all day and don't go outside.
B1: In my opinion, yes. Many teenagers have high screen time and are addicted to scrolling. It stops them from doing homework or sleeping well.
3. What are the advantages (good things) and disadvantages (bad things) of social media? ⚖️
Challenge learn new skills fake news cyberbullying
A2: A good thing is you can chat with friends. A bad thing is it hurts your eyes.
B1: On the one hand, social media is great for finding communities and learning new things. On the other hand, it can spread fake news and make people feel jealous of others' lives.
4. If you could invent a brand new app, what would it do? 💡
Creative solve a problem help students
A2: I want to invent an app for homework. It will do my math homework for me!
B1: If I could invent an app, it would be a "clothing matcher". You take a picture of your clothes, and the app tells you what to wear today based on the weather.

🎭 6. Breakout Room Task

👥 ZOOM TIP Create Breakout Rooms of 2-3 students. Set a timer for 8 minutes.

🔌 Situation: The "Unplug" Weekend Challenge

Goal: Student A wants to try a weekend with zero screens (no phones, no TV). They must convince Student B to join them. Student B isn't sure it's a good idea.

🗣️ Student A (The Challenger)

  • Suggest the "no screens" challenge.
  • Give reasons why it's healthy (sleep better, go outside).
  • Suggest fun alternative activities (sports, board games).

🤔 Student B (The Skeptic)

  • Listen and express doubt ("I don't know...", "But what if...").
  • Give reasons why you need your phone (chatting, music).
  • Try to compromise (e.g., "How about just 2 hours a day?").

A: Hey! Let's do a challenge this weekend. No phones, no screens at all. What do you think?

B: Are you crazy? A whole weekend? How will I talk to my friends?

A: We can hang out in person! We can go to the park or play basketball. It will be good to unplug.

B: I see your point, but I really want to play my new video game. Can we compromise?

🧑‍🏫 Teacher Support Box

Teaching Teens (Mixed A2-B1):

  • The "One-Word" Problem: Teens often give minimal answers ("Yes", "It's okay"). Always follow up with: "Tell me more about that" or "Why do you think so?"
  • Peer Pressure: They might be afraid of making mistakes in front of friends. Praise effort loudly: "That was a fantastic point!"
  • Differentiation: For A2 students, accept simple present sentences. For B1 students, push them to use "because", "but", and "for example".

Gentle Correction:

  • Instead of "No, that's wrong grammar", say: "I understand! A slightly more natural way to say that is..."
  • "You have a great idea there. Let's try saying it with our new word 'distracting'."
