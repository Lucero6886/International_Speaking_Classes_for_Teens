Today's Lesson Objectives:

  • Discuss the benefits and challenges of using AI for studying.
  • Use technology-related vocabulary to express ideas clearly.
  • Share personal opinions on academic honesty and AI tools.

1. Warm-Up Discussion 💬

Let's get started! Read the questions and share your thoughts.

1. Have you ever used an AI tool like ChatGPT? What did you use it for? 📱
"Yes, I used it once to help me find information about space for a science presentation."
2. What do you think is the best way to use the internet for homework? 🌐
"I think the best way is to search for explanations when you don't understand a topic, rather than just looking for the final answer."
3. Do your teachers allow you to use AI for school projects? 👨‍🏫
"Some teachers let us use it to get ideas, but we are not allowed to copy and paste the text into our essays."
4. What is one school subject where a computer could really help you? 💻
"I think it would really help in history, to quickly summarize long events and dates for me to study."
5. Do you think robots or AI will ever completely replace human teachers? 🤖
"No, I don't think so. AI is smart, but human teachers understand our emotions and can motivate us better."

2. Essential Vocabulary 📖

Learn these tech-related words to elevate your speaking skills today.

Generate
To create or produce something automatically.
"You can ask the AI to generate a list of ideas for your art project."
Plagiarism
Copying someone else's work (or AI's work) and calling it your own.
"Copying an entire essay from the internet is considered plagiarism."
Brainstorm
To think of many different ideas quickly before starting a project.
"We need to brainstorm topics for our science fair presentation."
Accurate
Correct, exact, and without any mistakes.
"You must check if the information provided by the AI is actually accurate."
Rely on
To depend on or trust something/someone for help.
"Students shouldn't rely on calculators for simple math problems."
Summarize
To make a long text shorter by giving only the main points.
"I asked the software to summarize the long historical article for me."
Evaluate
To judge how good, useful, or true something is.
"Teachers will evaluate your project based on creativity and effort."
Prompt
An instruction or question you type into an AI system.
"If you write a clear prompt, the AI will give you a much better answer."

3. Speaking Practice 🎙️

Choose your difficulty! Use the Idea Bank if you need inspiration.

Easy
1. How often do you use technology or the internet for your schoolwork?
  • Every day for research and typing essays.
  • Only on weekends when I have big projects.
  • Rarely, I prefer using physical textbooks.
"I use the internet almost every day to help me with my homework."
"I use technology daily for my schoolwork. Usually, I use my laptop to research topics for history or science. I also type all my essays on it because it's much faster than writing by hand."
Easy
2. What kind of homework is the hardest for you to do alone?
  • Math problems because the formulas are confusing.
  • Writing essays because it is hard to brainstorm ideas.
  • Learning a new language because of the grammar.
"Math homework is usually the hardest for me to do by myself."
"For me, writing long essays is the hardest task to do alone. I often struggle to brainstorm good ideas or organize my paragraphs. That's when I usually ask my parents or look online for inspiration."
Easy
3. If an AI could do one homework task for you, what would you choose?
  • Create a study schedule for my exams.
  • Summarize long reading chapters.
  • Check my spelling and grammar.
"I would ask AI to summarize my history reading chapters."
"If I could choose, I would use AI to check my grammar and spelling. I always make small mistakes when I write fast, so having a tool to fix those errors would save me a lot of time."
Medium
4. How can AI help you brainstorm ideas for a creative writing assignment?
  • It can generate different character names and personalities.
  • It can give you 5 different plot ideas to choose from.
  • It helps overcome "writer's block" by giving you a starting sentence.
"AI can generate unique character ideas and plot twists for my story."
"When I have writer's block, AI is great for brainstorming. I can type a simple prompt, and it will generate several different story plots. I don't copy the stories, but reading them gives me the inspiration to write my own."
Medium
5. Why is it very important to check the facts an AI gives you?
  • AI sometimes makes up information that sounds real.
  • The information might be outdated or old.
  • You need accurate facts to get a good grade on your test.
"It's important because AI can sometimes provide inaccurate or fake information."
"You must always evaluate the facts from an AI. Sometimes, these programs generate answers that look very confident but are actually completely wrong. If you include false information in your schoolwork, your teacher will lower your grade."
Medium
6. What is the difference between using AI for help and committing plagiarism?
  • Help: Asking AI to explain a hard concept so you can write it yourself.
  • Plagiarism: Copying the AI's exact paragraph and putting your name on it.
  • Help: Using it to organize your notes into an outline.
"Help is getting ideas, while plagiarism is copying the text exactly as your own."
"Using AI for help means you ask it to explain a math formula or summarize a text to understand it better. Plagiarism happens when you tell the AI to 'write my essay', and then you hand in that exact text. One helps you learn, the other is cheating."
Medium
7. How do you feel when you know a classmate uses AI to do all their work?
  • It feels unfair because I spend hours studying and they don't.
  • I don't mind, it's their choice and they won't learn anything.
  • I feel worried for them when we have a real exam without computers.
"I feel it's a bit unfair because I work hard, but they are not really learning."
"Honestly, it feels a little unfair if they get a good grade without trying. However, I know they aren't actually learning the material. When it comes time for a final exam in the classroom, they won't be able to rely on technology."
Opinion
8. In your opinion, should schools ban AI completely or teach students how to use it safely?
  • Teach it: AI is the future, so we need to know how to use it for jobs.
  • Teach it: If they ban it, students will just use it secretly anyway.
  • Ban it: It stops students from thinking critically.
"In my opinion, schools should teach us how to use it because it is part of our future."
"I strongly believe schools should teach students how to use AI properly instead of banning it. AI is a powerful tool that will be used in future jobs. If teachers show us how to use it for brainstorming and research without plagiarizing, it will be very beneficial."
Opinion
9. Do you think relying on AI makes students lazier or more creative? Why?
  • Lazier: People stop trying to solve hard problems themselves.
  • More creative: It gives you amazing starting ideas so you can build bigger projects.
  • Both: It depends on the student's attitude.
"I think it depends. It makes lazy students lazier, but it can help creative students get better ideas."
"From my perspective, it can do both. If a student relies on it to do all their work, they become lazy and lose their skills. But, if a student uses it to generate baseline ideas and then adds their own unique thoughts, it actually boosts their creativity."
Opinion
10. How do you think AI will change schools and classrooms in the next 10 years?
  • Every student will have a personalized AI tutor.
  • There will be fewer written essays and more speaking exams.
  • Teachers will focus more on project-based learning.
"I think in 10 years, every student will have their own AI tutor to help them study."
"In the future, I think AI will completely change homework. Because AI can write essays perfectly, teachers might stop giving written homework. Instead, classrooms will focus on speaking, presentations, and hands-on projects where we have to prove what we know in person."

4. Real-Life Roleplay 🎭

Act out these situations with a partner. Use the vocabulary you learned!

Scenario 1: The Confused Friend

Situation: Your friend is very stressed about a history essay due tomorrow. They want to use an AI to write the whole paper and hand it in. Explain why this is a bad idea and suggest a better way.

Roles: You & Your Stressed Friend.

Friend: "I have no time! I'm just going to let AI write my essay."

You: "I don't think you should do that. That is plagiarism and you could fail."

Friend: "But what else can I do? I have no ideas."

You: "Let's use a prompt to brainstorm ideas instead, and then you can write it in your own words."

Scenario 2: The Teacher Interview

Situation: You handed in an excellent science project. Your teacher is asking you how you completed it so fast. Explain how you used AI properly to organize your notes without cheating.

Roles: You (Student) & The Teacher.

Teacher: "This project is fantastic. How did you research this so quickly?"

You: "Thank you! I used an AI tool to help me summarize long articles."

Teacher: "Did the AI write the text for you?"

You: "No, I didn't rely on it for writing. I evaluated the facts it gave me and wrote my own conclusions."
