🍕 Friendship & Free Time

An A2/B1 Interactive English Speaking Lesson for Teens

🎯 Learning Objectives:
  • Use vocabulary related to hobbies and personality traits.
  • Describe your best friend and talk about shared activities.
  • Express opinions on what makes a good friendship.

1. Warm-up 🎈

Let's get talking! Read the questions and click to see an example answer.

🎮 1. What do you like to do on the weekend?

"On the weekend, I love sleeping in late and playing video games with my friends online."

🤝 2. Who is your best friend?

"My best friend is Nam. We have known each other since primary school."

⚽ 3. Do you prefer playing sports or playing video games?

"I prefer playing sports. I play basketball every Tuesday evening because it keeps me active."

😂 4. What makes you laugh the most?

"Watching funny animal videos on TikTok always makes me laugh a lot!"

🛍️ 5. Where is your favorite place to hang out?

"My favorite place to hang out is the shopping mall because we can eat fast food and look at clothes."

2. Key Vocabulary 🗣️

Review these words about friendship and hobbies. Click "See Example" and the Speaker icon 🔊 to practice!

Hang out (v.)
To spend time relaxing with friends.
"After school, I usually hang out with my friends at the park."
Trustworthy (adj.)
Someone you can believe and rely on completely.
"My best friend is very trustworthy; she never tells my secrets."
Hobby (n.)
An activity you do for fun in your free time.
"Drawing comic book characters is my favorite hobby."
Support (v.)
To help someone when they are having a hard time.
"Good friends will always support you when you feel sad."
Shared interests (n.)
Things that two or more people both like to do.
"We became friends because we have shared interests in music and gaming."
Argument (n.)
An angry disagreement between people.
"We had a big argument yesterday, but we apologized today."
Keep a secret (v.)
To not tell anyone something that is private.
"I told her who I liked because I know she can keep a secret."
Hilarious (adj.)
Very, very funny.
"My best friend always tells hilarious jokes during lunchtime."

3. Speaking Practice 💬

Practice answering these questions out loud. If you get stuck, open the Idea Bank and Sample Answers.

1. Describe your best friend. What do they look like? Easy
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Appearance: Tall, short, curly hair, wears glasses, always wears sneakers.
  • Personality: Funny, quiet, smart, very energetic.
"My best friend is tall, has short curly hair, and wears cool glasses."
"My best friend's name is Sarah. She is quite tall and has long straight hair. She usually wears jeans and sneakers. She is a very energetic person and is always smiling."
2. What do you and your friends usually do after school? Easy
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Activities: Go to a cafe, play sports, do homework together, play mobile games.
"After school, we usually go to a bubble tea shop and talk."
"Usually, right after school finishes, my friends and I walk to the bubble tea shop near the gate. We hang out there for an hour, drink tea, and complain about our homework before we go home."
3. How many close friends do you have? Easy
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Numbers: Just one best friend, a small group of three, a big squad.
  • Why: I prefer quality over quantity, I like knowing lots of people.
"I have a small group of three very close friends from my class."
"I know a lot of people at school, but I only have two really close friends. We tell each other everything. I prefer having a few true friends instead of many fake friends."
4. Why is it important to have friends? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Emotions: To not feel lonely, to have someone to share secrets with.
  • Support: They help you with problems, they cheer you up.
"It's important because friends support you when you have a problem."
"In my opinion, having friends is super important because life would be very boring and lonely without them. Good friends support you when you are sad, and they make happy moments even more fun. You need someone you can trust."
5. What do you usually talk about with your friends? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Topics: School gossip, favorite video games, famous singers, clothes.
"We usually talk about our favorite video games and funny things at school."
"My friends and I talk about almost everything! We usually discuss what happened in class or complain about our teachers. But mostly, we talk about our shared interests, like the new Marvel movies or our favorite video games."
6. How do you meet new friends? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Places: At school clubs, on sports teams, online in games.
  • Actions: Saying hello first, offering to share a snack, working on a group project.
"I usually meet new friends when we are put in the same group project at school."
"I am a bit shy, so I usually meet new friends through my school clubs or sports teams. For example, joining the basketball team helped me meet people from different classes. Sometimes I also make friends online while playing multiplayer games."
7. What is a fun memory you have with your best friend? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Events: A sleepover party, a school trip, getting lost in the mall.
  • Details: We laughed so hard, we stayed up until 3 AM, we ate too much pizza.
"A fun memory is when we had a sleepover and stayed up until 2 AM watching scary movies."
"My favorite memory with my best friend was our school trip to the beach last year. We sat together on the bus, sang songs very loudly, and shared our snacks. Later, we built a huge sandcastle. It was a hilarious and perfect day."
8. Do you think it is better to have one best friend or many normal friends? Opinion
💡 Idea Bank:
  • One best friend: Deeper connection, absolute trust, they know everything about you.
  • Many friends: More fun, never bored, always someone to play different games with.
"I think it's better to have one best friend because you can trust them with your secrets."
"I strongly believe it is better to have one true best friend. If you have many normal friends, you might have fun, but you can't tell them your deepest secrets. One best friend is totally trustworthy and will always support you when things are difficult."
9. Should friends always agree with each other? Opinion
💡 Idea Bank:
  • No: It's boring if everyone is the same, arguments help you understand each other, you should be honest.
  • Yes: Disagreeing causes fights, it's easier when you like the same things.
"No, I don't think so. Honest friends should tell you when you are wrong."
"From my point of view, friends shouldn't always agree. If you always agree, the friendship is a bit fake and boring. A real friend will tell you the truth, even if it causes a small argument. Learning to respect different opinions is part of a healthy friendship."
10. Can boys and girls be best friends without falling in love? Opinion
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Yes: They can just have shared hobbies like gaming or sports, they see each other like brother/sister.
  • No: People say it always gets complicated eventually.
"Yes, absolutely. They can just share the same hobbies and act like brother and sister."
"Yes, I guess that boys and girls can definitely be best friends! If they have shared interests, like a love for comic books or playing football, they can just hang out for fun. They can easily see each other as a brother or a sister without any romantic feelings."

4. Real-life Role-play 🎭

Grab a partner (or your teacher) and practice these conversational scenarios using the chat interface.

🛍️ Scenario A: Making Plans

Situation: You want to invite your friend to hang out at the mall this weekend. Send them a message, but they might be busy!

Student A: Bored Friend Student B: Busy Friend
Bored Friend: Hey! Are you free this Saturday? Let's hang out at the mall!
Busy Friend: Oh man, I would love to, but I have to visit my grandparents on Saturday.
Bored Friend: Ah, that's too bad. What about Sunday afternoon?
Busy Friend: Sunday afternoon works! We can get bubble tea and check out the new sneaker store.
Bored Friend: Perfect! Let's meet at the main gate at 2 PM.
Busy Friend: See you then! I'm so excited.

😔 Scenario B: Saying Sorry

Situation: You completely forgot your best friend's birthday yesterday. You need to apologize to them immediately.

Student A: Guilty Friend Student B: Sad Friend
Guilty Friend: Hey... I am so, so sorry. I completely forgot it was your birthday yesterday.
Sad Friend: Yeah, I noticed. I was waiting for you to call me all day.
Guilty Friend: I feel terrible. I had a huge math test and my mind was blank. Please forgive me!
Sad Friend: I was really sad, but I know you've been stressed about school. It's okay.
Guilty Friend: Thank you! Let me buy you lunch tomorrow to celebrate. My treat!
Sad Friend: Haha, okay deal. I want a large pizza!
