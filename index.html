🚦 Traffic Problems Near My School

👤 Target: Teens (13-16) 📊 Level: A2-B2 ⏱️ Time: 60 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • ✅ Use vocabulary related to traffic and daily commutes.
  • ✅ Answer personal questions about getting to school fluently.
  • ✅ Express simple opinions and propose solutions to city problems.

1. Warm-Up 🏃‍♂️

Answer these questions to get started. Click the button if you need ideas!

1. How do you usually get to school? 🚶‍♂️🚲🚗🚌

"I usually take the school bus, but sometimes my parents drive me."

2. How long does your journey take? ⏱️

"It usually takes about 20 minutes, but it can take 40 minutes if there is bad traffic."

3. Do you like your commute to school? Why/why not? 👍👎

"I don't really like it. The bus is always crowded and it's quite boring."

4. Are you ever late for school because of traffic? ⏰

"Yes, at least once a month! Especially when it rains, the roads get completely blocked."

5. What is the most annoying thing on the road? 😠

"The most annoying thing is when drivers honk their horns constantly for no reason."

2. Vocabulary 📚

Traffic jam
A long line of vehicles that cannot move forward.
We were stuck in a traffic jam for an hour this morning.
Rush hour
The busy part of the day when roads are crowded.
Try to avoid driving near the school during rush hour.
Pedestrian
A person walking in a city or along a road.
Drivers must stop for pedestrians at the crosswalk.
Commute
To travel regularly between school/work and home.
My daily commute to school is very stressful.
Exhaust fumes
Harmful gases produced by vehicle engines.
Walking to school is unpleasant because of the exhaust fumes.
Public transport
Buses, trains, and subways that anyone can use.
Taking public transport is better for the environment than driving.
Congestion
The state of being too crowded with traffic.
The city is building wider roads to reduce congestion.
Carpool
Sharing a car journey with other people.
My neighbors and I carpool to school to save time and gas.

3. Main Speaking Practice 🎙️

Click on the buttons to reveal ideas and sample answers to help you speak!

Level: Easy

Q1: Is the traffic heavy near your school in the morning?

  • Yes, very heavy / Lots of parents driving.
  • No, it's a quiet street / Most students walk.
Short: Yes, it is very heavy because many parents drop their kids off at the same time.
Extended: Yes, it gets really heavy between 7:30 and 8:00 AM. So many parents drive their kids to school, which creates a huge bottleneck right in front of the main gates.
Level: Easy

Q2: What kinds of vehicles do you see the most?

  • Cars / SUVs (parents dropping kids off).
  • Motorbikes / Scooters.
  • School buses / City buses.
Short: I mostly see private cars and motorbikes on my way to school.
Extended: I see a lot of motorbikes weaving through the traffic. There are also many large cars and SUVs. Sometimes there are a few yellow school buses, but it's mostly personal vehicles.
Level: Easy

Q3: Is it safe to cross the street near your school?

  • Yes, we have a crossing guard.
  • No, cars drive too fast.
  • Sometimes, you have to look carefully.
Short: Not really, you have to be very careful because cars don't always stop.
Extended: It can be quite dangerous. Even though we have a zebra crossing, drivers are always in a rush in the morning and sometimes ignore the pedestrians waiting to cross.
Level: Medium

Q4: What causes the most traffic congestion near your school?

  • Parents parking badly / double-parking.
  • Narrow streets.
  • Roadworks or construction nearby.
Short: I think the main cause is parents stopping right in front of the school gates.
Extended: The main cause is definitely parents double-parking to drop off their kids. It blocks a whole lane of traffic and forces everyone else to squeeze past, which slows everything down.
Level: Medium

Q5: How does bad traffic affect the students?

  • Makes them late for class.
  • Causes stress and anxiety in the morning.
  • Breathing in dirty air (pollution).
Short: It makes students late and stressed before the school day even begins.
Extended: Bad traffic makes a lot of students late for their first class. This starts the day off with stress. Also, walking through heavy traffic means breathing in unhealthy exhaust fumes.
Level: Medium

Q6: Do you think taking the bus is better than driving? Why?

  • Yes, one bus replaces many cars.
  • Yes, better for the environment.
  • No, buses are too slow or crowded.
Short: Yes, because one bus can carry many students, which reduces the number of cars.
Extended: Yes, I strongly believe taking the bus is better. One bus can carry about 40 students. That means up to 40 fewer cars on the road, which massively reduces both congestion and pollution.
Level: Medium

Q7: How would you describe the noise levels outside your school?

  • Very loud / deafening.
  • Lots of honking horns and engine noise.
  • Hard to concentrate in classrooms facing the street.
Short: It is extremely loud, especially with all the cars honking.
Extended: It is incredibly noisy. You constantly hear horns honking, engines revving, and traffic police blowing whistles. It makes it really hard to concentrate if our classroom windows are open.
Level: Opinion

Q8: What should the school do to improve the traffic situation?

  • Ban cars directly in front of the gates.
  • Stagger start times (e.g., Grade 9 starts at 8:00, Grade 10 at 8:15).
  • Encourage cycling or walking clubs.
Short: The school should create a "no car zone" right outside the gates.
Extended: I believe the school should enforce a "no car zone" directly in front of the entrance. They could also encourage staggered drop-off times so that not every student arrives at the exact same minute.
Level: Opinion

Q9: If you were the city mayor, how would you fix traffic jams?

  • Make public transport free for students.
  • Build protected, safe bike lanes.
  • Charge a toll/fee for driving in the city center.
Short: I would build safer bike lanes and make buses free for students.
Extended: If I were mayor, I would invest heavily in public transport and make it free for teenagers. I would also build protected bike lanes so that students feel safe cycling to school instead of needing their parents to drive them.
Level: Opinion

Q10: Do you think traffic will get better or worse in the future?

  • Worse: population is growing, more people buy cars.
  • Better: smart city technology, flying cars.
  • Better: more people might start working/studying from home.
Short: Probably worse, because the population is growing and more families own cars.
Extended: Unfortunately, I think it will get worse before it gets better. More families are buying private cars every year. Unless we completely change our habits and switch to green transport, the roads will just get more congested.

4. Role-Play Scenarios 🎭

Grab a partner or your teacher and act out these real-life situations.

Scenario A: Late for Class

Situation: You are 15 minutes late for your first class because of a terrible traffic jam. Your teacher asks why you are late.

Roles: Student & Teacher.

Student: "I'm so sorry I'm late, Mr./Ms. [Name]..."

Teacher: "Why are you arriving now? Class started 15 minutes ago."

Student: "There was a huge [traffic jam / accident / roadworks] on Main Street. The bus couldn't move."

Teacher: "You need to leave home earlier. Please take your seat."

Student: "I understand. I'll try to [catch an earlier bus / leave earlier] tomorrow."

Scenario B: The "Walk to School" Club

Situation: You want to start a club where students walk or cycle to school together to avoid traffic. You are trying to convince your skeptical friend to join.

Roles: Organizer & Skeptical Friend.

Organizer: "Hey, you should join my new Walk to School club! It's going to be great."

Friend: "Walk? No way, it takes too long and the traffic is dangerous."

Organizer: "But if we go in a group, it's safer. Plus, we won't get stuck in the car drop-off line."

Friend: "I don't know... I like sleeping in."

Organizer: "It's healthy, we get fresh air, and we can chat on the way. Just try it once on Friday!"
