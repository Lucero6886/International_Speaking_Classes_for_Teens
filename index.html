2. Vocabulary 📖

Words for staying safe online. Tap a card for an example.

password🔑
A secret word or code that protects your account.
"Never share your password with strangers online."
stranger🚶
A person you don't know.
"Don't give personal information to a stranger online."
privacy🔒
Keeping your personal information safe and secret.
"Turning off location sharing protects your privacy."
cyberbullying💢
Being cruel or mean to someone online.
"Reporting cyberbullying can help stop it."
block🚫
To stop someone from contacting you online.
"I blocked the account that sent weird messages."
report⚠️
To tell someone in charge about a problem.
"You should report fake or dangerous accounts."
scam🎣
A trick to steal money or information.
"That message about a free phone was a scam."
personal information🪪
Details like your name, address, or school.
"Never post personal information in public comments."
trustworthy🤝
Someone you can believe and rely on.
"Only add trustworthy people as friends online."
screenshot📸
A picture of what's on your screen.
"Take a screenshot if someone sends a scary message."

4. Role-Play 🎭

Act these out with a partner. Tap Show hints if you get stuck.

🎬 Scenario 1 — A message from a stranger

Student A just got a friend request and a message from someone they don't know. They talk to Student B, a close friend, about what to do.

🧑 Student A — You (got the message)🧑 Student B — Your friend
  • A: "Hey, a stranger just messaged me online. I don't know what to do."
  • B: "Really? What did they say?"
  • A: "They asked for my phone number and photos."
  • B: "Don't reply! You should block them and tell an adult."
  • A: "You're right. I'll block them now and tell my mum."

🎬 Scenario 2 — Talking to a parent about screen time

Student A wants more time on their phone tonight. Student B, a parent, is worried about safety and balance.

🧑 Student A — Teen👩 Student B — Parent
  • A: "Can I have more time on my phone tonight?"
  • B: "How much time have you already spent online today?"
  • A: "About two hours, but I want to play with my friends."
  • B: "Okay, but remember to use a strong password and never share personal information."
  • A: "I promise! I'll be careful and finish my homework first."
